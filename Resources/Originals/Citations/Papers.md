# Paper Citations

The four key papers are under traditional copyright. Some have author-hosted PDFs; others require library access (ACM Digital Library, IEEE Xplore).

## On the Criteria to Be Used in Decomposing Systems into Modules

- **Author**: D. L. Parnas
- **Year**: 1972
- **Venue**: Communications of the ACM, Vol. 15, No. 12, pp. 1053–1058
- **DOI**: 10.1145/361598.361623
- **ACM Digital Library**: <https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/361598.361623>
- **Free legal copy**: Various course pages host PDFs; Parnas himself has not released it under open license. Treat the ACM DL version as canonical.

## A Note on Distributed Computing

- **Authors**: Jim Waldo, Geoff Wyant, Ann Wollrath, Sam Kendall
- **Year**: 1994
- **Venue**: Sun Microsystems Laboratories Technical Report SMLI TR-94-29
- **Sun TR archive (Oracle)**: legacy URLs broken; technical report widely mirrored
- **Common course-hosted PDF**: <https://www.cs.princeton.edu/courses/archive/fall03/cs518/papers/waldo94.pdf> (Princeton COS-518 course copy — fair use for course material)
- **Note**: Sun's technical reports were never formally open-licensed. Oracle (post-acquisition) has not asserted enforcement, but the paper isn't redistributable in the strict legal sense.

## Out of the Tar Pit

- **Authors**: Ben Moseley, Peter Marks
- **Year**: 2006
- **Venue**: Software Practice Advancement (SPA) 2006 conference paper
- **Author-hosted PDF**: <http://curtclifton.net/papers/MoseleyMarks06a.pdf> (most-cited mirror; the authors did circulate it themselves but never published a formal license)
- **Note**: De-facto open in practice, but no explicit license. Don't redistribute aggressively.

## No Silver Bullet — Essence and Accident in Software Engineering

- **Author**: Frederick P. Brooks Jr.
- **Year**: 1986 (original) / 1987 (IEEE Computer reprint)
- **Venue**:
  - Original: *Information Processing 1986: Proceedings of the IFIP Tenth World Computing Conference*, pp. 1069–1076
  - Reprint: IEEE Computer 20(4):10–19 (April 1987)
- **IEEE Xplore**: <https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1663532>
- **Most-cited PDF mirror**: <http://worrydream.com/refs/Brooks-NoSilverBullet.pdf> (fair-use educational copy)
- **In print**: included as Ch. 16 of *The Mythical Man-Month* (anniversary edition), which is the most accessible canonical form.

## A note on "course PDFs"

Many of these papers exist as university course PDFs. Linking them is fine — these constitute fair-use educational mirrors. *Mirroring those mirrors* into a personal vault is a gray area; we link rather than copy.

## How to access for research

- **ACM Digital Library** — Parnas, many other CACM/Communications classics.
- **IEEE Xplore** — Brooks, embedded/aerospace papers.
- **Author homepages** — Kleppmann, Fowler, several others post their own PDFs.
- **Citeseer / Semantic Scholar** — search by DOI; many open versions surface.
